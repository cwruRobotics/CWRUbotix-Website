National Robotics Challenge

The National Robotcs Challenge (NRC) is multiple robotics competitions rolled into one event. Held nearby in Marion, Ohio in the beginning of April, the competitions are open to students in 6th grade through graduate school. In 2016, our teams participated in the Rescue, Combat, Maze, and Sumo competitions.

Combat

Two robots enter, one robot leaves. Saws, rammers, grabbers, flippers, and weapons of all kinds are all allowed in robot combat. Competition classes range from 100 grams to 220 pounds.
In April 2016, The Fish, a 3-pounder, competed at the National Robotics Challenge in Merion, OH. It won the bronze medal and was the only combat nominee for the Honda Innovation award. The Fish was built by Michael Schaffer, Hanyu Zhang, Johanna Fritzinger, Jonathan Mertz, George Hernandez, and Ailin Yu. It was laid to rest in the display cabinets on the third floor of Glennan.
